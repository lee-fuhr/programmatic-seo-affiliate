Best pressure washer for concrete and driveways (2025)

If you’re tackling stubborn oil stains, moss, or years of grime on a concrete driveway, the Simpson MSH3125 is our top pick. It delivers the best balance of cleaning power, durability, and value for homeowners and light commercial users who need a reliable machine that won’t quit mid-job.

Simpson MSH3125 – Top Pick

The Simpson MSH3125 pairs a Honda GX200 engine with a Simpson-branded axial cam pump, a combination known for long life and consistent pressure. Its 3,100 PSI at 2.5 GPM provides enough force to strip old sealer and clean deeply etched concrete without constant trigger fatigue. The steel frame and pneumatic tires make it easy to move over uneven driveways, and the included 5/8-inch hose resists kinking.

One real weakness: The stock spray wand’s quick-connect fitting can sometimes leak if not fully seated, and replacement o-rings are a minor nuisance over time.

Ideal buyer: Homeowners or handymen who want a workhorse machine capable of handling annual driveway deep-cleans and occasional fence or deck jobs without breaking down.

DeWalt DXPW60606

DeWalt’s DXPW60606 runs a 4.0 GPM flow rate at 4,200 PSI, making it one of the most powerful residential units available. The extra water volume helps rinse concrete pores thoroughly, and the triplex pump with pressure unloader adds durability for frequent, long sessions. DeWalt’s roll-cage frame protects the engine and pump during transport and storage.

One real weakness: It’s heavy—nearly 130 pounds—and the large tires don’t pivot, so maneuvering around tight corners or up stairs requires significant effort.

Ideal buyer: Heavy-duty DIYers or small contractors who clean multiple large concrete areas weekly and need commercial-grade flow and pressure without a trailer-mounted rig.

Generac 3100 PSI

The Generac 3100 PSI model uses a reliable OHV engine and an axial cam pump, delivering a steady 2.5 GPM at 3,100 PSI. It’s built around a compact, low-profile frame that stores easily, and the integrated soap tank is handy for pre-treating concrete stains. Generac’s “Spray MAX” nozzle system offers quick pattern changes without swapping tips.

One real weakness: The plastic pump housing can crack if exposed to freezing temperatures without proper winterization, and customer support for warranty claims has mixed reviews.

Ideal buyer: Homeowners on a budget who need a capable machine for seasonal driveway cleaning and want a smaller footprint for garage storage.

Sun Joe SPX4000

Sun Joe’s SPX4000 is an electric pressure washer that outputs 2.3 GPM at 2,030 PSI, which is modest for concrete but sufficient for light-duty driveways and patio slabs. Its brushless induction motor is quieter than gas units and requires no oil changes or fuel mixing. The included 35-foot hose and onboard storage for nozzles and cords keep the workspace tidy.

One real weakness: It lacks the pressure to remove deeply embedded oil stains or thick moss from rough concrete; you’ll need a surface cleaner attachment to get decent results.

Ideal buyer: Suburban homeowners with small driveways and limited storage space who prefer electric over gas and need a machine for light concrete cleaning plus general outdoor use.

Bottom line

For most people looking for the best pressure washer for concrete in 2025, the Simpson MSH3125 offers the sweet spot of power, reliability, and ease of use. If you need higher flow for heavy commercial-grade work, the DeWalt DXPW60606 is a beast—but be ready for its weight. The Generac 3100 PSI is a solid budget gas option for occasional use, while the Sun Joe SPX4000 works well for those who prefer electric and have smaller, less stained concrete surfaces. Whatever you choose, pair your machine with a surface cleaner attachment and the right nozzle to protect the concrete from etching or gouging.
