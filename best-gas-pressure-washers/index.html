Best gas pressure washers (2025): serious cleaning power compared

Our top pick is the Simpson Cleaning MSH3125 because it combines a proven Honda GX200 engine with a reliable triplex plunger pump, offering the best blend of long-term durability and everyday cleaning muscle for most homeowners and light commercial users.

Simpson Cleaning MSH3125

The MSH3125’s genuine strength is its no-compromise build. The Honda GX200 engine is famously bulletproof, and the triplex plunger pump holds up far better than the axial pumps found on many comparably priced machines. You get consistent pressure for stripping paint, cleaning heavy concrete stains, and blasting away ground-in grime without frequent pump failures. The frame is welded steel with a solid cart design that rolls well even over rough ground.

The real weakness is the price of replacement parts and the occasional difficulty finding a local authorized service center for the Honda engine. Also, at roughly 80 pounds, it’s not the lightest unit to lift into a truck bed.

If you want a pressure washer that will still be running strong in five years and you don’t mind paying a little more upfront, the Simpson MSH3125 is the clear choice for serious home use and contractor-lite jobs.

Generac 3100 PSI

Generac’s 3100 PSI model delivers a great price-to-performance ratio, often coming in under $400 during sales. The engine starts reliably (most units use a Generac OHV engine), and the axial cam pump is adequate for occasional residential tasks like cleaning driveways, decks, and siding. The integrated hose reel and onboard soap tank are genuinely handy for keeping your workspace organized.

The weakness is that the axial pump is more prone to wear if you let the machine sit with water in it or run it without proper inlet filters. Also, the plastic frame and handle can feel a bit flimsy compared to steel-framed rivals.

The Generac 3100 PSI is ideal for the budget-conscious homeowner who needs basic cleaning power for a few weekends each year and isn’t looking for a professional-grade workhorse.

DeWalt DXPW60606

The DeWalt DXPW60606 stands out for its premium axial cam pump (the AAA pump) and the brand’s extensive nationwide service network. It offers a solid 3.0 GPM at 3400 PSI, which means fast cleaning for large surfaces like long driveways or farm equipment. The hose is a braided wire-reinforced model that resists kinking, and the trigger gun is ergonomic with a lock-off feature for safety.

The weakness is that the AAA pump, while better than generic axial pumps, still isn’t as robust as a triplex pump, and the price tag is higher than many competitors with similar specs. Some users also find the wheels a bit small for rough terrain.

The DeWalt DXPW60606 is best for the DIY enthusiast who values brand support, wants strong flow for large-area cleaning, and is okay with a high-quality axial pump rather than a triplex.

Excell 3100 PSI

The Excell 3100 PSI pressure washer is often overlooked but offers genuine value for the spec-obsessed buyer. It features a Honda GCV160 engine (same basic powerplant used on many reliable mowers) and a triplex plunger pump at a price that undercuts most Honda-powered competitors. The cart frame is welded steel with pneumatic tires, making it easy to move over gravel or grass.

The weakness is that Excell is a less established brand, which means parts and customer support can be hit-or-miss depending on where you buy. Also, the trigger gun and wand feel a little cheaper than those on a Simpson or DeWalt unit.

The Excell 3100 PSI is a smart pick for the budget-minded buyer who insists on a Honda engine and a triplex pump but doesn’t need the name recognition — or the higher price tag — of the Simpson.

Honda GCV160 (Pressure Washer with Honda GCV160 Engine)

Pressure washers built around the Honda GCV160 engine (often sold under various brands like Ryobi or Powerstroke) give you Honda’s legendary easy-start and reliable operation in a compact, lightweight package. These units are typically around 2600–3000 PSI, making them great for lighter residential tasks like washing cars, patios, and fences. The GCV160 engine is quiet, fuel-efficient, and easy to maintain with screw-in valve adjusters.

The weakness is that these washers almost always use an axial cam pump, which limits longevity compared to triplex-pump models. And because the GCV160 is a smaller engine, you won’t get the flow or pressure needed for heavy concrete cleaning or stripping multiple coats of paint.

If your primary need is a light- to medium-duty washer that starts every time, sips gas, and you value Honda reliability over raw power, a GCV160-based unit is a smart, affordable choice.

Bottom line: For most people who need real cleaning power for a mix of home and occasional commercial work, the Simpson Cleaning MSH3125 earns the “best gas pressure washer” title thanks to its durable triplex pump and Honda GX200 engine. If your budget is tighter and your tasks are lighter, the Generac 3100 PSI or a GCV160-based model will serve you well. The DeWalt DXPW60606 offers strong flow with great support, while the Excell 3100 PSI is the value king in the Honda-powered triplex space. Match the machine to your actual workload — a triplex pump pays off if you’ll use it regularly, but an axial pump is fine for seasonal cleaning.
